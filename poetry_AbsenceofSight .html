Creative Writing - Poetry

In the Absence of Sight

Awards received: HONORABLE MENTION (Scholastic), December 2017

she was a two-dimensional girl; if she turned just right,
she would cast no shadow at all.
her mind bled onto the brittle pages of books;
dozens of pages drenched in glistening ink,
each volume gradually darkening with brittle rot and neglect.

when the clamor in her skull shrank to a murmur,
she painted her fingernails the color of bitter wine
and dug them into her arms in celebration.
the ache coaxed some sliver of feeling
from the numbness she so dearly despised,
so she curled into herself;
clinging desperately to her own skin
and staring into the face of the moon
until the static crept back through her lungs.

she tucked herself into the corners of places
where smoke hung thick and heavy,
and people never met your gaze.
she bared slim hips and the curve of her neck
in an attempt to peel away the layers of guilt
    and see what lay beneath.
but each dawn
they would only thicken in a stifling film over her lips.
senseless, she stumbled home -
rib cage bruised,
reeking of emptiness and disgust.

the mirror revealed a wasted thing.
defeat had gnawed away blocks of confidence,
smudged shadows beneath dull eyes,
laced tremors through slender wrists
that hung suspended by silver thread.
    and yet,

silence.
    a scream.
        the shatter of dust-coated glass.
the girl had fallen upwards:
clawing for purchase in the clouds,
drowning in the vastness of her own atmosphere.
cities blurred to an amber haze as she slowed,
suspended within a hallucination
painted too vividly to let go.

it was an overwhelming serenity.
breaths of lavender dusted the horizon,
hiding rich swaths of violet draped over foreign shores.
the moon was a pool of silver
framed in gently rounded light.
but when the girl reached to brush the doubt from her lashes,
her fingertips skimmed across twin hollows
brimming with ink.
a chill traced down her spine.

the stars leapt past her, giggling as the moon plummeted to meet the sea.
    wait, she had cried. what about my eyes?
but the world faded to black again, hours turning creamy and soft.
fear had torn her from above.
fear of flowers wilting at her windowsill
    and vacant bedrooms
        and scorch marks on her tongue.
too late she had realized, that by giving up her last breath
she had fogged up all the hope in the sky.

sunlight spilled over the floorboards, smooth and sweet
as the girl mused at the lines of her reflection.
no one had eyes like hers -
lost in realms unknown to gravity
and tangled in constellations.
no one bled clear beams of moonlight
or exhaled trails of stardust.
she was alone
    and yet,
she was infinite.

human, the girl said quietly.
    that is the last thing I could be.
