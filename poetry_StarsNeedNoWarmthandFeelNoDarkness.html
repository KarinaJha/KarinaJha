Stars Need No Warmth and Feel No Darkness

July 2019

The stars, of course, need no poetry to know their beauty.
Just as a rich man has no need to assert his wealth,
constellations do not bother to check their reflection in human eyes.
With the ability to create a steady, self-sustaining stream of light,
all forms of relief are effectively rendered unnecessary.
Mortal direction is so often guided by the cosmos,
both in physical and moral navigation.
Ships turn to chase the brightest rays,
and hearts catch onto their sails to follow suit.
Rationality is led into submission
when faced with the radiance of the night sky.
Still, humans are fools to love anything born of apathy.
The stars allow themselves to be tracked only by the disciplined calculation of time;
any sentimental pursuit falls short, for celestial bodies can feel no attachment.
They live off the vastness of the universe in place of emotional provision.
